polyval

returns the value of a polynomial of degree n evaluated at x. y = polyval(p,x,[],mu) or [y,delta] = polyval(p,x,S,mu) use x^=(x−μ1)/μ2 in place of x. In this equation, μ1=mean(x)and μ2=std(x). The centering and scaling parameters mu = [μ1,μ2] is optional

Calling Sequence

y= polyval(p,x)
[y,delta] = polyval(p,x,S)

Parameters

p:

vector of length n+1 whose elements are the coefficients in descending powers of the polynomial to be evaluated.

x:

-can be a matrix or a vector. In either case, polyval evaluates p at each element of x.

delta:

-is an estimate of the standard deviation of the error in predicting a future observation at x by p(x).

Examples

p = [3 2 1];
y=polyval(p,[5 7 9])
//EXPECTED OUTPUT:
//y=  86  162  262
